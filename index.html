About Me

Talofa Lava! My name is Lest, welcome to my WDD131 homepage. Here, I share my course projects and practice my web development skills. I'm currently in my third term as an online student at BYU-Idaho, working toward my first certificate in Web & Computer Programming which is part of the path to earning my associate degree in Software Development.

I was born in Torrance, California, and raised in Salt Lake City, Utah, where I spent most of my life. Growing up in the U.S. gave me access to many educational and technological opportunities, which I've come to appreciate even more after relocating. Today, I live in the beautiful island of Samoa - a place rich in culture, tradition, and community values. Living here has given me a new perspective on the importance of technology in connecting people and supporting local development. Even though it's far from the fast pace of big cities, Samoa has so much potential, and I'm proud to call it home.

One of my biggest goals is to take the skills I'm learning through my Web & Computer Programming studies and apply them in ways that benefit small businesses across Samoa. Many local entrepreneurs here have great products and services but limited online presence, which can hold them back from reaching a wider audience. I hope to support these businesses by helping them build websites, improve their digital visibility, and tap into the power of online tools and platforms. I truly believe that even basic digital solutions can create meaningful growth and open new doors for communities like ours.
